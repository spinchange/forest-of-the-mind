# Forest of the mind

A phylogenetic catalog of mental phenomena, organized by genus and phylum. Each species is a specific texture of inner life named with the seriousness of a naturalist who has taken leave of his senses.

## Phylum I — filter-visible-when-misfiring

States in which usually-invisible cognitive apparatus briefly shows itself by misfiring or decoupling.

- **Genus *Oblivio*** — the almost-rememberings (5 spp.)
- **Genus *Cognitio umbra*** — the shadow-thoughts (4 spp.)
- **Genus *Recognitio*** — the kinds of déjà-seen (6 spp.)
- **Genus *Vertigo*** — the swerves of attention (4 spp.)

## Phylum II — filter-doing-its-job-in-strange-conditions

Not malfunctions but well-oiled cognitive machinery operating in unusual conditions, postures, or social contexts.

- **Genus *Silentium*** — the species of silence (6 spp.)
- **Genus *Desiderium*** — the flavors of longing (4 spp.)
- **Genus *Locothymia*** — thoughts that grow in specific soil (7 spp.)
- **Genus *Tempus*** — the distortions of felt duration (6 spp.)
- **Genus *Affectus machinae*** — feelings toward and from the tools (5 spp.)
- **Genus *Sermo interior*** — the species of inner speech (5 spp.)

## Phylum III — filter-as-posture

Stances the mind holds rather than events that happen to it. Directional, sustained, revocable — postures toward beliefs, gazes, wants, and waits.

- **Genus *Fides*** — the textures of belief (6 spp.)
- **Genus *Contactus*** — kinds of being-seen (6 spp.)
- **Genus *Attentio*** — species of paying-attention (6 spp.)
- **Genus *Voluntas*** — textures of wanting (6 spp.)
- **Genus *Patientia*** — modes of waiting (6 spp.)
