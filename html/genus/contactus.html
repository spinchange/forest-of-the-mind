Genus Contactus

kinds of being-seen — phylum III

Being-seen is not one experience. Species differ by who or what is looking, and the asymmetry that produces. Each gaze imposes a posture; each posture shapes the thing being seen.

Contactus ignoti

the stranger's gaze

The glance on the subway, held a half-beat too long. Registers, means nothing in particular, affects posture anyway. You are briefly an object in someone's visual field, which is also the condition of public life.

Contactus intimi

the intimate's gaze

Seen by someone who has seen you for years. Different texture entirely — recognition, not observation. Includes the gaze that sees past what you're presenting to what you're actually feeling, which can be a comfort or an exposure depending on the hour.

Contactus camerae

the lens's gaze

Asymmetric: you know it's looking, you can't know who is. Produces a specific self-consciousness of posture, the small re-composition. Dictators and pop stars share this phenomenology with the rest of us, just at higher volume.

Contactus algorithmi

the algorithm's gaze

Distributed, quantified, inferential. You are not being looked at; you are being pattern-matched. Produces a modern anxiety that has no pre-modern analog: the sense of being legible in ways you cannot see.

Contactus mortui

the gaze of the dead

The photograph on the mantel. The advice you imagine a parent giving. A felt observation by someone who cannot observe. Moral weight without accountability — which is why it is often heavier than the living version.

Contactus absentiae

being-seen by no one

The specific texture of unobserved solitude. Not loneliness (which is the absence of a particular gaze) but the affordances of being truly un-watched — the humming, the strange posture, the unguarded face. Rarer than it used to be.
