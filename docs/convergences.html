Convergences

The genera give the vertical structure; the convergences give the horizontal. Two species from different genera can share a deep trait — the same underlying phenomenon wearing different clothing. Marsupials and placentals both evolved wolves; the mind similarly reinvents its shapes.

the known-unknown

Members

  • Oblivio linguarum — genus Oblivio
  • Silentium machinae — genus Silentium

Shared trait. Something is present in the system and is not coming out. The held-up process. The blinking cursor in both silicon and mind.

presence-of-just-ended

Members

  • Oblivio dulcis — genus Oblivio
  • Silentium post musicam — genus Silentium

Shared trait. Both are shaped like the thing that left them. Not absence, but a lingering outline of what was.

the filter, briefly exposed

Members

  • Cognitio umbra nominis — genus Cognitio umbra
  • Vertigo speculi — genus Vertigo
  • Recognitio inversa — genus Recognitio
  • Affectus codicis mortui — genus Affectus machinae

Shared trait. Normally invisible cognitive machinery briefly reveals itself by misfiring or decoupling. Four habitats (hearing, mirror, language, code) with one shared phenomenology: the self as stranger.

longing purified by inaccessibility

Members

  • Desiderium loci numquam visi — genus Desiderium
  • Desiderium temporis alieni — genus Desiderium

Shared trait. Sister species. The impossibility of satisfaction is not an obstacle to the longing — it is the fuel.

the occupied channel

Members

  • Locothymia balnei — genus Locothymia
  • Sermo interior musicus — genus Sermo interior

Shared trait. Verbal-analytical cognition gets its breakthrough precisely because something else is using the loud part of the brain. The insight arrives through a service entrance.

inverse codec bias

Members

  • Tempus feriarum — genus Tempus
  • Desiderium temporis alieni — genus Desiderium

Shared trait. Inverse operations on memory. One compresses real time into smaller remembered time; the other inflates never-lived time into richly remembered time. Memory as a lossy codec with configurable bias.

the unsaid doing the work

Members

  • Sermo interior dialogicus — genus Sermo interior
  • Silentium offensum — genus Silentium

Shared trait. The conversation that never happens out loud, and the refusal that is itself a statement. Both do their work in the register of the withheld.

the threshold as switch

Members

  • Oblivio liminis — genus Oblivio
  • Locothymia liminis inversa — genus Locothymia

Shared trait. Sister species sharing a habitat (the doorway) with opposite metabolisms: one forgets because of the threshold, the other remembers at it.

time uncoupled from obligation

Members

  • Locothymia vigiliae tertiae — genus Locothymia
  • Tempus nuntii mali — genus Tempus
  • Vertigo scalarum — genus Vertigo

Shared trait. The 3am mind, the bad-news second, and the scale-swerve share a substrate: time with nowhere to be, expanding accordingly.

posture laid down without ceremony

Members

  • Fides mortua — genus Fides
  • Patientia mortua — genus Patientia

Shared trait. The signature of Phylum III: a stance you had been holding for a long time is discovered to have ended some time ago. No decision-moment, no crossing-over. The holding simply stopped, and consciousness is late to the news.

the un-watched affordance

Members

  • Contactus absentiae — genus Contactus
  • Silentium nivis — genus Silentium

Shared trait. The un-watched and the un-heard both license a strange freedom. The humming, the strange posture, the unguarded face — things that only appear when the observational field goes quiet.

scales refused

Members

  • Attentio vigil — genus Attentio
  • Vertigo scalarum — genus Vertigo

Shared trait. Opposite postures with the same substrate: the mind refusing to stay at normal scale. One scans outward for threat; the other zooms out until the threat becomes abstract.

the shape of what is no longer there

Members

  • Voluntas velle — genus Voluntas
  • Desiderium futuri perditi — genus Desiderium

Shared trait. Sister postures: the first a stance toward a lost want, the second the grief of a lost future. Both are about the shape of something that is no longer fully present, held in awareness anyway.
